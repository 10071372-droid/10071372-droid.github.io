Designing spaces, tools and interfaces that hold vulnerable bodies with care.

My work moves between hospital wards, tactile AI interfaces and speculative forensic toolkits. I am interested in how architecture and interaction design can reframe everyday systems for people whose needs are often overlooked.

Selected projects

Three different scales of care

  • A soft, squeezable interface that translates embodied gestures into AI prompts, challenging purely text-based creative workflows. Squish explores how people who find text-heavy tools exhausting or inaccessible might enter AI-assisted creation through touch, pressure and rhythm.
    Embodied interaction Generative AI Alternative input
  • Ethnographic research inside stroke rehab wards mapping how privacy, surveillance, noise and circulation shape patients’ psychological recovery. The work proposes spatial strategies for micro-privacy, softer observation and more agency within highly controlled medical environments.
    Healthcare design Ethnography Patient experience
  • A speculative toolkit for future forensic investigators, including DNA-based tracking, bias detectors and mind-reading headsets. Instead of promising technological neutrality, the project asks how tools might expose their own politics and force investigators to confront their assumptions at the crime scene.
    Critical design Future scenarios Tool ethics

More works (hospital tools, domestic violence spatial research, colour-blind urban wayfinding, etc.) can be shared on request.

Background

Practice between architecture and interaction

I trained in architecture and interdisciplinary studies at The Bartlett (UCL), and I am currently developing an interaction-driven design practice focusing on healthcare, disability and vulnerable bodies in institutional spaces.

  • Research on stroke rehab environments, privacy and surveillance in hospital design.
  • Projects around disability, accessibility and everyday tools, often informed by lived experience.
  • Work across physical prototyping, spatial mapping, storytelling and speculative design.
